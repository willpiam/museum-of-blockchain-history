# Museum of Blockchain History

## Bitcoin

- **From Satoshi to Hal**
  - On January 12, 2009, Satoshi Nakamoto sent 10 BTC to Hal Finney in Bitcoin's first recorded peer-to-peer transaction, proving the network worked beyond mining rewards.
  - Transactions:
    - [`f4184fc596403b9d638783cf57adfe4c75c605f6356fbc91338530e9831e9e16`](https://www.blockchain.com/explorer/transactions/btc/f4184fc596403b9d638783cf57adfe4c75c605f6356fbc91338530e9831e9e16)

- **Pizza Purchase**
  - This transaction is associated with the famous Bitcoin Pizza Day purchase in which Laszlo Hanyecz spent 10,000 BTC for two pizzas on May 22, 2010.
  - Transactions:
    - [`a1075db55d416d3ca199f55b6084e2115b9345e16c5cf302fc80e9d5fbf5d48d`](https://www.blockchain.com/explorer/transactions/btc/a1075db55d416d3ca199f55b6084e2115b9345e16c5cf302fc80e9d5fbf5d48d)

## Ethereum

- **Hu Lezhi Brain-Computer Weapons Burn**
  - Someone identifying as Hu Lezhi burned hundreds of ETH and attached a message accusing executives at a Chinese hedge fund of using 'brain-computer weapons'. The Eth was burned presumably to bring attention to the message.
  - Transactions:
    - [`0x5e8bef5dcb69206fa1bacc8d0b0c0204e12f1e45483d12b9f69dc1829ac74315`](https://etherscan.io/tx/0x5e8bef5dcb69206fa1bacc8d0b0c0204e12f1e45483d12b9f69dc1829ac74315)
